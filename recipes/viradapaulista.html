VIRADA À PAULISTA

Virada à Paulista is a whole-meal dish from the state of São Paulo, Brazil. It dates to the time of the earliest Portuguese settlers to the region.

The dish consists of a platter of beans, dried and toasted manioc flour, pork chop, fried sausage, breaded and fried plantain, rice, fried eggs, cooked pork rind and fried collards

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of cooked beans, blended in a blender;
  • 2 cloves of garlic;
  • 1 onion;
  • Salt to taste;
  • 1 cup of manioc flour;
  • 2 tablespoons of soybean oil or olive oil;
  • 3 cups of water;
  • 2 pork chop;
  • 1/2 kilogram of sausage;
  • 2 plantain;
  • 2 egg;
  • 1 cup of milk;
  • 1 bunch of finely fried collards;
  • 200 grams of cooked and fried pork rind;
  • Cooked white rice.

Steps

Beans
  1. Sauté the onion and garlic;
  2. When the onion is transparent, add the beans and let it boil for about 10 minutes;
  3. Add two cups of water and add salt to taste;
  4. Add 1/2 cup of manioc flour;
  5. Add two cups of water and add salt to taste;
  6. Stir until it takes on the appearance of a thick mush, then add to the cooked white rice, the fried pork rind and the finely fried collards.
Pork Chop
  1. Add salt to taste;
  2. Then, in a preheated pan add the soybean oil or olive oil, the pork chop and fry with the pan covered, adding water as it dries;
  3. After about 30 minutes, remove from the heat and serve as an accompaniment to the beans.
Sausage
  1. Cook the sausages in boiling water for 20 minutes;
  2. Fry in a preheated pan;
  3. Serve as an accompaniment to the beans and pork chop.
Fried Plantain
  1. Cut the bananas in half, pass through the beaten egg and milk, then pass through 1/2 cup of manioc flour;
  2. Fry in soybean oil or olive oil;
  3. Serve as an accompaniment to the beans, pork chop and sausage.
Fried Egg
  1. Just fry egg rss;
  2. Serve as an accompaniment to the beans, pork chop, sausage and fried plantain.
